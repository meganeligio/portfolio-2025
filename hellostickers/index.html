Student Project

hello stickers

  • UX/UI
  • E‐commerce

Overview

hello stickers was a concept store that I made showcasing my own illustrations in cute sticker forms. I've always had a knack for pastel and adorable things, and the idea of a fun sticker shop has continuously lingered in the back of my mind. I had yet to pursue it because it would take the time and effort I didn't have. If I were to in the future, this would be my ideal design.

homepage hero section

Problem

There were many competitors when it came to selling stickers, so I wanted to stand out by incorporating my personal flavor. I also wanted visuals that were pleasing to the eye and key features that would help the store gain exposure in a positive way. Customers should also have a seamless browsing experience.

early prototype

First Draft by Yours Truly

Creating an E‐commerce Website

In the first step towards designing an e‐commerce website, I created a prototype using a template that was pre‐populated with content. Moreover, I reviewed the UX and information architecture prior to designing in order to gain a better understanding of the original designer's choices. Using this prototype, I continued to build upon it with the main priority being user‐friendliness.

ideation

Brand Style Guide

Color Palette: Cute Raspberry

I went with a color scheme that embodied the cute and simplistic nature of my stickers while also being easy on the eyes.

Logo Design

I went with a smiling sun as a logo because it represented the essence of my stickers which is brightening people's days.

Solution

I designed an easy‐to‐navigate website that included reviews for authenticity and a blog where customers could have fun discussions. By implementing these features, it would promote the legitimacy of the store, securing the trust of potential customers, and also build a community where everyone is welcome.
